THE GRASSLANDS

Along the western side of the Sierra Nevada Mountain Range lied a massive grassland. These grasslands were made extremely fertile by the Sierra's rivers and streams that flowed through these plains towards the coast. These grasslands rivaled the Great Plains and had a huge population of grazing animals. However, since this land was so agriculturally rich, by the turn of the century, much of this land was destroyed due to agriculture. Desertification spread and now all that remains are isolated and fragmented areas of land, making this one of the most endangered habitats in the world.
