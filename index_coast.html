THE COAST

California borders the pacific ocean and much of our culture is shaped by the sea. The sea fog is crucial to the health of our redwood forests which consume most of their water through their coniferous leaves rather than through the ground. The sea fog is so immense that it reaches the western part of the Sierra Nevadas where the Sequoia Tree, a species of redwood, uses this fog to survive as well. California has many rivers that flow into the ocean, most of which are used as spawning grounds for many species of fish that migrate thousands of miles in the open ocean during their lives. The ocean supports us with its bounty of fish and countless other resources, however, the most poluted waters in all of the United States are in parts of California. The Bay Area has been having massive shark and ray die offs due to polluted run off that has been entering the waters. There is so much we can do to conserve our waters and enact more sustainable practices.
