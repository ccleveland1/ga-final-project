THE RIVER

California's interconnected water system serves over 30 million people and irrigates over 5,680,000 acres of farmland. As the world's largest, most productive, and most controversial water system, sit manages over 40,000,000 acre feet of water per year. Our state is a huge contributing source to the global salmon and steelhead population, yet we have over 1,400 damns that block the natural path of fish migration and spawning. What is even more alarming is that a majority of these damns are over 50 years old and serving no purpose. They are just negatively affecting the river ecosystem and need to be destroyed. A small movement has been growing where people have been breaking down unnecessary damns.
