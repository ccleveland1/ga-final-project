OUR GOAL

California is a vast and abundant state. We boast several different ecosystems and habitats. The variety extends from the old growth forests in the north to the nutrient rich grasslands in the central valleys. The desert plains in the south and east and the great Sierra Nevadas, the backbone of the state. California is arguably one of the environmentally richest and diverse states in all of America. However, it is greatly in danger. With 222 endangered species and redwood forests that are endemic to this state and crucially at risk. Our state is a huge contributing source to the global salmon and steelhead population, yet we have over 1,400 damns that block the natural path of fish migration and spawning. 20% of California is National Forest yet ess than 4 percent of the original extent of virgin redwood forests remain, and only 2.5 percent of this is protected. California's grasslands are considered one of the most endangered habitats in the world due to agricultural developement. Californias population is concentrated along the coast making the remaining wild areas either fragmented or at risk of encroaching developement.
