California rivers are extremely important to the biodiversity of the environment.
They contribute greatly to the global steelhead, salmon and other fish specie populations since these rivers are used for spawning.
These rivers also deliver precious sediment and nutrients downstream to trees and plants that need them to thrive.
The river is a vital part of the entire ecosystem from mammals, fish to birds and insects.
With more than 80% of the worlds forests already destroyed, environmental conservation is more important than it has ever been.
