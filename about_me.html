ABOUT ME

I grew up wondering what happens to the wilderness and to the earth when humans do something harmful and what happens when humans spread their reach globally. THE FOREST

Deforestation is a complex problem, but one with real world solutions. California is the only state where redwoods can grow yet due to deforestation, only 4% of the virgin redwood forests still exist and only 2% of it is protected. 20% of California is National Forest yet we are not fighting to keep this vast area of land that is so crucial to our state healthy and intact. Fragmentation is segregating many migratory species and populations of animals. 80% of California's water comes from our Forest. When a forest is cut down, desertification becomes rampant and dust bowls occur. When desertification occurs, global temperature and ecosystems become destroyed. The important role our forest has trickles down to every single ecosystem. We cannot have a healthy state without a healthy forest.

THE RIVER

California's interconnected water system serves over 30 million people and irrigates over 5,680,000 acres of farmland. As the world's largest, most productive, and most controversial water system, sit manages over 40,000,000 acre feet of water per year. Our state is a huge contributing source to the global salmon and steelhead population, yet we have over 1,400 damns that block the natural path of fish migration and spawning. What is even more alarming is that a majority of these damns are over 50 years old and serving no purpose. They are just negatively affecting the river ecosystem and need to be destroyed. A small movement has been growing where people have been breaking down unnecessary damns.
